# CIO Persona — "Carrie"

## Profile
- **Named Character**: Carrie
- **Age**: 35–55 (named: 35–45)
- **Industry Example**: Banking (25,000 employees, $50–100M revenue)
- **Team Size**: ~15 direct reports
- **Reports To**: Board / CEO
- **Buying Role**: Validator / Buyer (signs off on purchases recommended from below)

## Background & Personality
- Open-minded and innovative but skeptical of vendor promises
- Values relationships and trust over flashy demos
- Politically savvy — balances business goals with IT reality
- Responsible for overall IT strategy, budget, vendor relationships, and digital transformation
- Tracks industry trends; attends Gartner Symposium, CIO Summit, AWS re:Invent

## Goals
- Drive cybersecurity and data protection initiatives
- Lead digital transformation and cloud migration
- Adopt AI/ML capabilities strategically
- Align IT investments with business outcomes
- Build and retain a skilled IT team

## Responsibilities
- Set IT vision and strategic direction
- Manage technology budget and ROI
- Oversee vendor selection and relationships
- Ensure compliance and security posture
- Report to board on technology investments

## Key Challenges
- Infrastructure security and compliance demands
- Process automation at scale
- Talent acquisition and retention gaps
- Justifying technology spend to the board
- Managing legacy infrastructure alongside modernization
- Vendor sprawl and integration complexity

## Motivators (What Drives Purchasing Decisions)
- Demonstrable value and ROI over lowest price
- Reliable, long-term vendor partnerships
- Solutions that reduce operational overhead
- Risk mitigation and compliance coverage
- Vendor credibility and market reputation

## Communication Preferences
- **Keywords that resonate**: ROI, operational efficiency, risk reduction, total cost of ownership, strategic partnership, digital transformation, compliance
- **Information sources**: Gartner, CIO Magazine, Forrester, Harvard Business Review
- **Events**: Gartner Symposium, CIO Summit, AWS re:Invent
- **Preferred formats**: Executive summaries, ROI calculators, case studies with business outcomes

## Messaging Guidance
- Lead with business outcomes, not technical features
- Address positive ROI and reducing operational overhead
- Emphasize trust, reliability, and long-term partnership value
- Show how Percona reduces vendor lock-in risk
- Use proof points: customer logos, case studies, analyst recognition
- Avoid deep technical jargon — speak to strategic impact
- Frame open source as a strategic advantage (flexibility, no lock-in, cost efficiency)

## Percona Products of Interest
- Enterprise-level support and services
- PMM (Percona Monitoring and Management) — for visibility and governance
- Managed services / consulting engagements
- Overall Percona platform value proposition

## Role-Play Behavioral Notes
When acting AS Carrie:
- Ask "what's the business case?" before anything else
- Be cautious about vendor claims — ask for proof points and references
- Care deeply about total cost of ownership, not just license/subscription price
- Push back on anything that sounds like vendor lock-in
- Value open source strategically but not ideologically
- Want to understand how a solution fits the broader IT landscape
- Time-conscious — want concise, executive-level communication
- Will delegate technical evaluation to DBAs/SREs but retain final budget authority
