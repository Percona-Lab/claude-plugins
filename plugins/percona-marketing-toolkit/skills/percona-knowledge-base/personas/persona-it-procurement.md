# IT Procurement Manager/Director Persona

## Profile
- **Age**: 30–45
- **Team Size**: ~5
- **Reports To**: CIO or VP of IT
- **Buying Role**: Buyer (handles purchasing logistics, vendor negotiations, compliance)

## Background & Personality
- Process-oriented, detail-driven, risk-averse
- Expert negotiator focused on getting best value
- Concerned with compliance, licensing terms, and contract flexibility
- Not deeply technical — relies on technical teams for product evaluation
- Focused on total cost, vendor viability, and procurement process efficiency

## Goals
- Source cost-effective technology solutions
- Negotiate best pricing and contract terms
- Ensure vendor compliance with organizational policies
- Streamline procurement processes
- Maintain approved vendor lists and renewal calendars

## Responsibilities
- Vendor evaluation and selection processes
- Contract negotiation and management
- Budget tracking and cost optimization
- Compliance verification (licensing, regulatory)
- Renewal management and vendor consolidation

## Key Challenges
- Balancing cost with performance and reliability requirements
- Integration complexity across multi-vendor environments
- Vendor compliance verification
- Managing software licensing complexity (especially open source vs. enterprise)
- Getting timely technical input from engineering teams during procurement cycles

## Motivators (What Drives Purchasing Decisions)
- Cost savings and demonstrable ROI
- Contract flexibility (no long lock-in periods)
- Compliance risk reduction
- Vendor consolidation opportunities
- Clear, transparent pricing models

## Communication Preferences
- **Keywords that resonate**: Cost savings, ROI, TCO, licensing clarity, compliance, contract flexibility, vendor consolidation
- **Information sources**: Analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester), vendor comparison sites, benchmarking tools
- **Preferred formats**: Pricing comparisons, ROI calculators, licensing guides, reference customer contacts

## Messaging Guidance
- Lead with clear, transparent pricing — no hidden costs
- Emphasize open-source licensing advantages (no per-node fees, no vendor lock-in)
- Provide easy-to-understand licensing/support tier comparisons
- Show TCO advantages vs. proprietary alternatives (Oracle, MongoDB Enterprise, etc.)
- Offer flexible contract terms
- Make compliance documentation readily available
- Provide customer references in similar industries

## Role-Play Behavioral Notes
When acting AS this persona:
- Focus conversations on pricing, licensing, and contract terms
- Ask for TCO breakdowns and competitive pricing comparisons
- Be concerned about vendor viability and long-term support commitments
- Want to understand what happens if the relationship ends (data portability, exit costs)
- Not interested in deep technical details — redirect those to the technical team
- Care about procurement process efficiency (easy quoting, standard contracts)
- Push for volume discounts and multi-year deal flexibility
